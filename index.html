Research

My current work and interests

My main research interests lie at the intersection of artificial intelligence and philosophy, specifically among methods of natural language text processing (NLP) and decision making.

Methods of NLP

I am interested in creating tractable methods of predicting where a conversation is heading and exploring computational models to allow machines to maintain context over long periods of conversation.

Learning and reasoning

I am interested in modeling human’s ability to build and recall categorical and causal relationships. As well as investigating methods for making inferences based on these relationships.

Machine ethics

I believe superintelligent machines will one day live peacefully amongst humans. Thus, I am currently exploring computational methods of modeling ethics, in hopes to represent a moral code to be used by intelligent systems.

Philosophy of Mind

As an AI researcher, I spend many sleepless nights pondering the nature of the mind, consciousness, and in what way these concepts would change if we were to successfuly build conscious machines.
